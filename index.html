Prosthesis Sensation Features

Sensory feedback

Current prostheses do not give you a sense of touch. Some new prostheses with implanted stimulators may:

  • Give you a sense of touch in your fingertips and palm.
  • Give you a sense of temperature in your fingertips and palm.
  • Let you feel where your fingers and wrist are when you move them.

Intuitiveness of sensation

Some new prostheses may give you a sense of touch. However, where you feel the sense of touch may vary.

  • For some new prostheses, the feeling may be indirect. For example, you may feel touch on your upper arm when your prosthesis fingertip has touched something.
  • For other new prostheses, the feeling may be direct and intuitive. For example, you may feel the fingertip touch on your fingertip.

Recalibration frequency

You may need to run a program so the prosthesis gives you the correct sense of touch. Some new prostheses may need to be recalibrated once per day or less. Other new prostheses may need to be recalibrated twice per day or more.
